Chapter 2 – Board Games in Ancient Civilizations

2.1 Ancient Egypt

One of the most famous ancient Egyptian games was Senet, played on a grid of 30 squares. It was often placed in tombs, suggesting it had both recreational and spiritual significance.

2.2 Mesopotamia

The Royal Game of Ur, discovered in the city of Ur, is one of the oldest known board games. It featured a distinctive cross-shaped board and was played with dice-like objects.

2.3 The Indus Valley

Archaeologists have found small terracotta game pieces and patterned boards in Indus Valley sites, indicating early board games were common in South Asia as well.

Key Takeaways

  • Board games have existed in many ancient cultures worldwide.
  • Games often had both entertainment and cultural significance.
