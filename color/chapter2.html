Chapter 2 – Light and the Spectrum

2.1 The Nature of Light

Light is a form of electromagnetic radiation. It travels in waves, and the wavelength of light determines the color we perceive.

2.2 Visible Spectrum

The visible spectrum is the range of light wavelengths that the human eye can detect—approximately 380 to 750 nanometers.

  • Violet – shortest wavelength (~380 nm)
  • Blue
  • Green
  • Yellow
  • Orange
  • Red – longest wavelength (~750 nm)

Key Takeaways

  • Different wavelengths correspond to different colors.
