Chapter 2 – The Science Behind Chocolate

2.1 What’s in a Cacao Bean?

Cacao beans are packed with compounds that give chocolate its unique flavor, texture, and aroma:

  • Cocoa solids – Provide flavor and color
  • Cocoa butter – Gives smoothness and mouthfeel
  • Theobromine & caffeine – Provide mild stimulation

2.2 Tempering – The Crystal Science

Chocolate tempering is the process of carefully melting and cooling chocolate so the cocoa butter forms stable crystals. Proper tempering ensures:

  • Shiny, attractive appearance
  • Snappy texture when broken
  • Resistance to melting in your hands

2.3 Melting Points of Chocolate Types

Chocolate Type Approx. Melting Point (°C)
White Chocolate 40–42°C
Milk Chocolate 40–45°C
Dark Chocolate 45–50°C

2.4 Practice Exercise

  1. Melt small samples of white, milk, and dark chocolate in separate bowls.
  2. Use a thermometer to record the temperature at which each begins to melt.
  3. Compare your results to the table above.

Key Takeaways

  • Chocolate’s qualities come from a mix of chemistry and craftsmanship.
  • Tempering is essential for high-quality chocolate.
  • Different types of chocolate have different melting behaviors.
